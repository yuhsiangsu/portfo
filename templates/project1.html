Introduction

On March 11, 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared novel coronavirus (COVID-19) as a global pandemic. Until April 20, 2020, there are more than 2,000,000 infected cases and more than 150,000 deaths around the world. Although not being part of the WHO, Taiwan reported the first confirmed infected case on January 21, 2020, and started taking a series of precaution to prevent the virus from spreading out. Three months later (April 21, 2020), Taiwan has 422 infected cases, 6 deaths and 189 recovery cases.

In this project, the data analysis of COVID-19 is based on a case study of Taiwan. There are three sections in the project,

  1. A brief overview of the COVID-19 in Taiwan and its trend
  2. Distribution of the COVID-19 confirmed cases based on different categories
  3. The relations between total confirmed cases, deaths and recovery cases
